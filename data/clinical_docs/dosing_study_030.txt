DOSE-FINDING STUDY REPORT
Document ID: DFS-0030
Study Title: Dose-Response Evaluation of Semaglutide in Coronary Artery Disease

OBJECTIVE
To determine the optimal therapeutic dose of Semaglutide for the treatment of Coronary Artery Disease by evaluating efficacy and safety across multiple dose levels.

METHODS
This was a Phase IIb dose-ranging study. 406 patients with Coronary Artery Disease were randomized to receive Semaglutide at doses of 20mg, 25mg, or 200mg daily, or placebo, for 24 weeks.

PHARMACOKINETIC PROFILE
Semaglutide demonstrated linear pharmacokinetics across the dose range studied:
- Tmax: 3.5 hours
- Half-life: 8.0 hours
- Bioavailability: 64%
- Steady-state achieved by Day 10
- No significant food effect observed

DOSE-RESPONSE RESULTS
20mg: Modest improvement over placebo (effect size: 0.12, p=0.088)
25mg: Significant improvement (effect size: 0.51, p=0.0037) — RECOMMENDED DOSE
200mg: Similar efficacy to 25mg but with increased adverse events (effect size: 0.57, p=0.0012)

SAFETY BY DOSE
Treatment-emergent adverse events increased with dose:
- 20mg: 25% of patients
- 25mg: 32% of patients
- 200mg: 43% of patients
- Placebo: 10% of patients

RECOMMENDATION
The optimal dose of Semaglutide for Coronary Artery Disease is 25mg once daily, providing the best balance of efficacy and tolerability. Dose adjustment may be needed for patients with renal impairment (eGFR < 30 mL/min).
