CLINICAL TRIAL REPORT
Document ID: CTR-0008
Study Title: Phase II Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Empagliflozin for Atrial Fibrillation

STUDY OVERVIEW
This was a phase ii, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial evaluating the efficacy and safety of Empagliflozin in patients with Atrial Fibrillation. The study was conducted across 28 clinical sites in 9 countries.

PATIENT POPULATION
A total of 1285 patients were enrolled and randomized 1:1 to Empagliflozin or placebo. Key inclusion criteria included age 18-75 years with confirmed diagnosis of Atrial Fibrillation. The mean age of participants was 47 years, with 63% female patients. 68% of patients were White, 12% Black or African American, and 14% Hispanic or Latino.

STUDY DESIGN
Patients received Empagliflozin at a dose of 10mg twice daily or matching placebo for 36 weeks. The primary endpoint was improvement in Atrial Fibrillation composite score. Secondary endpoints included safety assessments, quality of life measures (SF-36), and time to first clinical event.

RESULTS
Primary Endpoint: Empagliflozin significantly improved the primary endpoint compared to placebo (p=0.0154, effect size=0.38, 95% CI [0.31, 0.49]).

The treatment group showed a mean change from baseline of -1.92 compared to -0.31 in the placebo group.

Safety Profile:
The most common adverse events in the Empagliflozin group were:
- nausea: 14.2% (vs 9.4% placebo)
- constipation: 2.2% (vs 1.4% placebo)
- fatigue: 1.4% (vs 0.5% placebo)

Serious adverse events occurred in 2.7% of the Empagliflozin group vs 1.5% of the placebo group. 0 deaths occurred during the study, none considered related to the study drug.

CONCLUSION
Empagliflozin demonstrated statistically significant efficacy in the treatment of Atrial Fibrillation. The safety profile of Empagliflozin was consistent with prior studies, with no new safety signals identified. Based on these results, Empagliflozin represents a promising therapeutic option for patients with Atrial Fibrillation.
