DOSE-FINDING STUDY REPORT
Document ID: DFS-0029
Study Title: Dose-Response Evaluation of Dapagliflozin in Obesity

OBJECTIVE
To determine the optimal therapeutic dose of Dapagliflozin for the treatment of Obesity by evaluating efficacy and safety across multiple dose levels.

METHODS
This was a Phase IIa dose-ranging study. 324 patients with Obesity were randomized to receive Dapagliflozin at doses of 15mg, 50mg, or 150mg daily, or placebo, for 12 weeks.

PHARMACOKINETIC PROFILE
Dapagliflozin demonstrated linear pharmacokinetics across the dose range studied:
- Tmax: 1.2 hours
- Half-life: 21.4 hours
- Bioavailability: 79%
- Steady-state achieved by Day 13
- No significant food effect observed

DOSE-RESPONSE RESULTS
15mg: Modest improvement over placebo (effect size: 0.15, p=0.141)
50mg: Significant improvement (effect size: 0.44, p=0.0052) — RECOMMENDED DOSE
150mg: Similar efficacy to 50mg but with increased adverse events (effect size: 0.46, p=0.0033)

SAFETY BY DOSE
Treatment-emergent adverse events increased with dose:
- 15mg: 15% of patients
- 50mg: 34% of patients
- 150mg: 44% of patients
- Placebo: 20% of patients

RECOMMENDATION
The optimal dose of Dapagliflozin for Obesity is 50mg once daily, providing the best balance of efficacy and tolerability. Dose adjustment may be needed for patients with hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B or C).
