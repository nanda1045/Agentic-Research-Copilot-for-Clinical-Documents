ADVERSE EVENT SURVEILLANCE REPORT
Document ID: AER-0023
Drug: Empagliflozin
Reporting Period: Q1 2022

SUMMARY
This post-marketing surveillance report summarizes adverse event data for Empagliflozin collected through the FDA Adverse Event Reporting System (FAERS) and manufacturer safety databases. A total of 8617 adverse event reports were analyzed during the reporting period.

ADVERSE EVENT DISTRIBUTION
The following adverse events were reported:
  - insomnia: 175 reports (2.03%), 7 serious
  - headache: 405 reports (4.7%), 51 serious
  - cough: 452 reports (5.25%), 65 serious
  - weight gain: 432 reports (5.01%), 0 serious
  - hypoglycemia: 346 reports (4.02%), 69 serious
  - gastrointestinal discomfort: 246 reports (2.85%), 26 serious

SIGNAL DETECTION
A new safety signal was identified for hypoglycemia which exceeded the proportional reporting ratio (PRR) threshold of 2.0 (observed PRR: 3.92).

RISK-BENEFIT ASSESSMENT
The overall benefit-risk profile of Empagliflozin remains favorable based on current data. Continued monitoring is recommended.

DEMOGRAPHICS OF REPORTERS
- Age range: 21-78 years (median: 54)
- Sex: 49% Female, 49% Male, 4% Not reported
- Geographic distribution: 53% North America, 15% Europe, 15% Asia-Pacific
