COMPARATIVE EFFECTIVENESS STUDY
Document ID: CES-0048
Study Title: Head-to-Head Comparison of Metformin vs Sitagliptin for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

BACKGROUND
Both Metformin and Sitagliptin are approved for the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. This study aimed to compare their relative efficacy and safety in a real-world clinical setting.

STUDY DESIGN
Multicenter, randomized, active-controlled, open-label trial. 2145 patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus were randomized 1:1 to Metformin or Sitagliptin for 48 weeks.

PATIENT BASELINE CHARACTERISTICS
- Mean age: 50 years
- Mean BMI: 30.0 kg/m²
- Mean disease duration: 4.2 years
- Prior treatment failure: 35%
- Concomitant medications: 68% on at least one

PRIMARY RESULTS
Metformin: Effect size 0.54 (95% CI [0.44, 0.64])
Sitagliptin: Effect size 0.42 (95% CI [0.32, 0.52])

Between-group difference: 0.12 (p==0.04)

Metformin demonstrated superior efficacy compared to Sitagliptin on the primary endpoint.

SAFETY COMPARISON
Treatment discontinuation due to adverse events:
- Metformin: 7.7%
- Sitagliptin: 8.5%

Notable differences in adverse event profiles:
- Metformin was associated with higher rates of joint pain (8.7% vs 2.1%)
- Sitagliptin was associated with higher rates of peripheral edema (12.7% vs 3.7%)

CONCLUSION
In this head-to-head comparison, Metformin demonstrated statistically significant superiority over Sitagliptin for the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. The safety profiles of both drugs were generally acceptable, though they differed in specific adverse event patterns. Treatment selection should consider individual patient factors and tolerability preferences.
