DOSE-FINDING STUDY REPORT
Document ID: DFS-0027
Study Title: Dose-Response Evaluation of Amlodipine in Chronic Kidney Disease

OBJECTIVE
To determine the optimal therapeutic dose of Amlodipine for the treatment of Chronic Kidney Disease by evaluating efficacy and safety across multiple dose levels.

METHODS
This was a Phase IIb dose-ranging study. 252 patients with Chronic Kidney Disease were randomized to receive Amlodipine at doses of 5mg, 25mg, or 200mg daily, or placebo, for 24 weeks.

PHARMACOKINETIC PROFILE
Amlodipine demonstrated linear pharmacokinetics across the dose range studied:
- Tmax: 1.0 hours
- Half-life: 6.9 hours
- Bioavailability: 95%
- Steady-state achieved by Day 13
- No significant food effect observed

DOSE-RESPONSE RESULTS
5mg: Modest improvement over placebo (effect size: 0.29, p=0.057)
25mg: Significant improvement (effect size: 0.63, p=0.0034) — RECOMMENDED DOSE
200mg: Similar efficacy to 25mg but with increased adverse events (effect size: 0.56, p=0.0046)

SAFETY BY DOSE
Treatment-emergent adverse events increased with dose:
- 5mg: 16% of patients
- 25mg: 30% of patients
- 200mg: 52% of patients
- Placebo: 16% of patients

RECOMMENDATION
The optimal dose of Amlodipine for Chronic Kidney Disease is 25mg twice daily, providing the best balance of efficacy and tolerability. Dose adjustment may be needed for patients with renal impairment (eGFR < 30 mL/min).
