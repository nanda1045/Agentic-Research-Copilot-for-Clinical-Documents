CLINICAL TRIAL REPORT
Document ID: CTR-0004
Study Title: Phase II Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Amlodipine for Chronic Kidney Disease

STUDY OVERVIEW
This was a phase ii, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial evaluating the efficacy and safety of Amlodipine in patients with Chronic Kidney Disease. The study was conducted across 118 clinical sites in 14 countries.

PATIENT POPULATION
A total of 2357 patients were enrolled and randomized 1:1 to Amlodipine or placebo. Key inclusion criteria included age 18-75 years with confirmed diagnosis of Chronic Kidney Disease. The mean age of participants was 50 years, with 51% female patients. 67% of patients were White, 8% Black or African American, and 8% Hispanic or Latino.

STUDY DESIGN
Patients received Amlodipine at a dose of 100mg once daily or matching placebo for 52 weeks. The primary endpoint was improvement in Chronic Kidney Disease composite score. Secondary endpoints included safety assessments, quality of life measures (SF-36), and time to first clinical event.

RESULTS
Primary Endpoint: Amlodipine significantly improved the primary endpoint compared to placebo (p=0.035, effect size=0.45, 95% CI [0.39, 0.55]).

The treatment group showed a mean change from baseline of -2.35 compared to -0.04 in the placebo group.

Safety Profile:
The most common adverse events in the Amlodipine group were:
- weight gain: 8.4% (vs 5.1% placebo)
- nausea: 2.0% (vs 0.7% placebo)
- muscle pain: 3.2% (vs 1.6% placebo)

Serious adverse events occurred in 3.2% of the Amlodipine group vs 1.4% of the placebo group. 3 deaths occurred during the study, none considered related to the study drug.

CONCLUSION
Amlodipine demonstrated statistically significant efficacy in the treatment of Chronic Kidney Disease. The safety profile of Amlodipine was consistent with prior studies, with no new safety signals identified. Based on these results, Amlodipine represents a promising therapeutic option for patients with Chronic Kidney Disease.
