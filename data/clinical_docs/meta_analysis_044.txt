META-ANALYSIS REPORT
Document ID: MAR-0044
Title: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Amlodipine for Chronic Kidney Disease

OBJECTIVE
To synthesize evidence from randomized controlled trials (RCTs) evaluating the efficacy and safety of Amlodipine in patients with Chronic Kidney Disease.

SEARCH STRATEGY
Electronic databases (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library) were searched from inception to June 2024. 1362 records were screened, and 13 RCTs meeting inclusion criteria were included in the final analysis.

INCLUDED STUDIES
- Number of RCTs: 13
- Total patients: 29251
- Study duration range: 12 to 53 weeks
- Phase II trials: 64%
- Geographic coverage: 25 countries

POOLED RESULTS
Primary Outcome:
- Pooled effect size (random-effects model): 0.68 (95% CI [0.21, 0.77])
- p-value: <0.001
- I² heterogeneity: 38% (moderate)
- Number needed to treat (NNT): 19

Safety Outcomes:
- Risk of serious adverse events: OR 1.1 (95% CI [0.68, 1.29])
- Treatment discontinuation: OR 1.41 (95% CI [0.87, 1.58])

SUBGROUP ANALYSES
- Age ≥65 years: Similar benefit (interaction p=0.61)
- Severe disease at baseline: Greater benefit (effect size: 0.8)
- Prior treatment failure: Maintained efficacy (effect size: 0.51)

CONCLUSION
This meta-analysis of 13 RCTs involving 29251 patients provides moderate evidence supporting the efficacy of Amlodipine for Chronic Kidney Disease. The safety profile is acceptable though ongoing surveillance is warranted.

QUALITY ASSESSMENT
- Risk of bias: Low to moderate (Cochrane RoB 2 tool)
- GRADE certainty of evidence: High
- Publication bias: Possible (Egger test p=0.049)
