COMPARATIVE EFFECTIVENESS STUDY
Document ID: CES-0037
Study Title: Head-to-Head Comparison of Semaglutide vs Canagliflozin for Coronary Artery Disease

BACKGROUND
Both Semaglutide and Canagliflozin are approved for the treatment of Coronary Artery Disease. This study aimed to compare their relative efficacy and safety in a real-world clinical setting.

STUDY DESIGN
Multicenter, randomized, active-controlled, open-label trial. 800 patients with Coronary Artery Disease were randomized 1:1 to Semaglutide or Canagliflozin for 24 weeks.

PATIENT BASELINE CHARACTERISTICS
- Mean age: 54 years
- Mean BMI: 31.4 kg/m²
- Mean disease duration: 9.8 years
- Prior treatment failure: 21%
- Concomitant medications: 43% on at least one

PRIMARY RESULTS
Semaglutide: Effect size 0.44 (95% CI [0.34, 0.54])
Canagliflozin: Effect size 0.83 (95% CI [0.73, 0.93])

Between-group difference: 0.39 (p=<0.001)

Canagliflozin demonstrated superior efficacy compared to Semaglutide on the primary endpoint.

SAFETY COMPARISON
Treatment discontinuation due to adverse events:
- Semaglutide: 10.1%
- Canagliflozin: 5.2%

Notable differences in adverse event profiles:
- Semaglutide was associated with higher rates of gastrointestinal discomfort (12.9% vs 1.8%)
- Canagliflozin was associated with higher rates of elevated liver enzymes (7.1% vs 1.9%)

CONCLUSION
In this head-to-head comparison, Canagliflozin demonstrated statistically significant superiority over Semaglutide for the treatment of Coronary Artery Disease. The safety profiles of both drugs were generally acceptable, though they differed in specific adverse event patterns. Treatment selection should consider individual patient factors and tolerability preferences.
