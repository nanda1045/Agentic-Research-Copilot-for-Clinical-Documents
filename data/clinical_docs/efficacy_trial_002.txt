CLINICAL TRIAL REPORT
Document ID: CTR-0002
Study Title: Phase III Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Lisinopril for Hypertension

STUDY OVERVIEW
This was a phase iii, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial evaluating the efficacy and safety of Lisinopril in patients with Hypertension. The study was conducted across 60 clinical sites in 11 countries.

PATIENT POPULATION
A total of 4722 patients were enrolled and randomized 1:1 to Lisinopril or placebo. Key inclusion criteria included age 18-75 years with confirmed diagnosis of Hypertension. The mean age of participants was 65 years, with 54% female patients. 81% of patients were White, 12% Black or African American, and 15% Hispanic or Latino.

STUDY DESIGN
Patients received Lisinopril at a dose of 250mg once daily or matching placebo for 36 weeks. The primary endpoint was change in systolic blood pressure (8.0 mmHg reduction vs placebo). Secondary endpoints included safety assessments, quality of life measures (SF-36), and time to first clinical event.

RESULTS
Primary Endpoint: Lisinopril significantly reduced the primary endpoint compared to placebo (p=0.0235, effect size=0.65, 95% CI [0.54, 0.72]).

The treatment group showed a mean change from baseline of -0.9 compared to -0.64 in the placebo group.

Safety Profile:
The most common adverse events in the Lisinopril group were:
- fatigue: 7.9% (vs 3.6% placebo)
- insomnia: 8.5% (vs 5.9% placebo)
- diarrhea: 1.7% (vs 0.9% placebo)

Serious adverse events occurred in 3.2% of the Lisinopril group vs 2.9% of the placebo group. 0 deaths occurred during the study, none considered related to the study drug.

CONCLUSION
Lisinopril demonstrated statistically significant efficacy in the treatment of Hypertension. The safety profile of Lisinopril was consistent with prior studies, with no new safety signals identified. Based on these results, Lisinopril represents a promising therapeutic option for patients with Hypertension.
