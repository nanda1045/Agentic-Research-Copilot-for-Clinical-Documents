META-ANALYSIS REPORT
Document ID: MAR-0045
Title: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Omeprazole for Heart Failure

OBJECTIVE
To synthesize evidence from randomized controlled trials (RCTs) evaluating the efficacy and safety of Omeprazole in patients with Heart Failure.

SEARCH STRATEGY
Electronic databases (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library) were searched from inception to March 2024. 632 records were screened, and 9 RCTs meeting inclusion criteria were included in the final analysis.

INCLUDED STUDIES
- Number of RCTs: 9
- Total patients: 5503
- Study duration range: 9 to 96 weeks
- Phase III trials: 69%
- Geographic coverage: 30 countries

POOLED RESULTS
Primary Outcome:
- Pooled effect size (random-effects model): 0.36 (95% CI [0.27, 0.84])
- p-value: <0.001
- I² heterogeneity: 18% (moderate)
- Number needed to treat (NNT): 11

Safety Outcomes:
- Risk of serious adverse events: OR 1.05 (95% CI [0.77, 1.52])
- Treatment discontinuation: OR 1.06 (95% CI [0.97, 1.53])

SUBGROUP ANALYSES
- Age ≥65 years: Similar benefit (interaction p=0.21)
- Severe disease at baseline: Greater benefit (effect size: 0.96)
- Prior treatment failure: Maintained efficacy (effect size: 0.64)

CONCLUSION
This meta-analysis of 9 RCTs involving 5503 patients provides moderate evidence supporting the efficacy of Omeprazole for Heart Failure. The safety profile is acceptable though ongoing surveillance is warranted.

QUALITY ASSESSMENT
- Risk of bias: Moderate (Cochrane RoB 2 tool)
- GRADE certainty of evidence: Moderate
- Publication bias: Not detected (Egger test p=0.6)
