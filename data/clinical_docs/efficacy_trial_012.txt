CLINICAL TRIAL REPORT
Document ID: CTR-0012
Study Title: Phase IV Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Losartan for Hypertension

STUDY OVERVIEW
This was a phase iv, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial evaluating the efficacy and safety of Losartan in patients with Hypertension. The study was conducted across 21 clinical sites in 12 countries.

PATIENT POPULATION
A total of 796 patients were enrolled and randomized 1:1 to Losartan or placebo. Key inclusion criteria included age 18-75 years with confirmed diagnosis of Hypertension. The mean age of participants was 58 years, with 63% female patients. 63% of patients were White, 15% Black or African American, and 7% Hispanic or Latino.

STUDY DESIGN
Patients received Losartan at a dose of 500mg twice daily or matching placebo for 48 weeks. The primary endpoint was change in systolic blood pressure (10.2 mmHg reduction vs placebo). Secondary endpoints included safety assessments, quality of life measures (SF-36), and time to first clinical event.

RESULTS
Primary Endpoint: Losartan significantly reduced the primary endpoint compared to placebo (p=0.0235, effect size=0.66, 95% CI [0.54, 0.81]).

The treatment group showed a mean change from baseline of -1.19 compared to 0.15 in the placebo group.

Safety Profile:
The most common adverse events in the Losartan group were:
- fatigue: 4.3% (vs 2.8% placebo)
- peripheral edema: 9.6% (vs 5.2% placebo)
- upper respiratory infection: 4.4% (vs 2.6% placebo)

Serious adverse events occurred in 3.0% of the Losartan group vs 3.4% of the placebo group. 3 deaths occurred during the study, none considered related to the study drug.

CONCLUSION
Losartan demonstrated statistically significant efficacy in the treatment of Hypertension. The safety profile of Losartan was consistent with prior studies, with no new safety signals identified. Based on these results, Losartan represents a promising therapeutic option for patients with Hypertension.
