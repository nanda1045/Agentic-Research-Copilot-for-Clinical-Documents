COMPARATIVE EFFECTIVENESS STUDY
Document ID: CES-0049
Study Title: Head-to-Head Comparison of Metformin vs Sitagliptin for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

BACKGROUND
Both Metformin and Sitagliptin are approved for the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. This study aimed to compare their relative efficacy and safety in a real-world clinical setting.

STUDY DESIGN
Multicenter, randomized, active-controlled, open-label trial. 514 patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus were randomized 1:1 to Metformin or Sitagliptin for 24 weeks.

PATIENT BASELINE CHARACTERISTICS
- Mean age: 58 years
- Mean BMI: 25.9 kg/m²
- Mean disease duration: 10.8 years
- Prior treatment failure: 38%
- Concomitant medications: 56% on at least one

PRIMARY RESULTS
Metformin: Effect size 0.4 (95% CI [0.3, 0.5])
Sitagliptin: Effect size 0.76 (95% CI [0.66, 0.86])

Between-group difference: 0.36 (p=<0.001)

Sitagliptin demonstrated superior efficacy compared to Metformin on the primary endpoint.

SAFETY COMPARISON
Treatment discontinuation due to adverse events:
- Metformin: 9.5%
- Sitagliptin: 8.9%

Notable differences in adverse event profiles:
- Metformin was associated with higher rates of gastrointestinal discomfort (8.3% vs 3.3%)
- Sitagliptin was associated with higher rates of gastrointestinal discomfort (14.2% vs 2.2%)

CONCLUSION
In this head-to-head comparison, Sitagliptin demonstrated statistically significant superiority over Metformin for the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. The safety profiles of both drugs were generally acceptable, though they differed in specific adverse event patterns. Treatment selection should consider individual patient factors and tolerability preferences.
