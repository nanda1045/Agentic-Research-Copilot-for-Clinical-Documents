DOSE-FINDING STUDY REPORT
Document ID: DFS-0025
Study Title: Dose-Response Evaluation of Lisinopril in Hypertension

OBJECTIVE
To determine the optimal therapeutic dose of Lisinopril for the treatment of Hypertension by evaluating efficacy and safety across multiple dose levels.

METHODS
This was a Phase IIa dose-ranging study. 221 patients with Hypertension were randomized to receive Lisinopril at doses of 10mg, 15mg, or 50mg daily, or placebo, for 16 weeks.

PHARMACOKINETIC PROFILE
Lisinopril demonstrated linear pharmacokinetics across the dose range studied:
- Tmax: 3.3 hours
- Half-life: 21.1 hours
- Bioavailability: 61%
- Steady-state achieved by Day 11
- No significant food effect observed

DOSE-RESPONSE RESULTS
10mg: Modest improvement over placebo (effect size: 0.15, p=0.05)
15mg: Significant improvement (effect size: 0.62, p=0.0085) — RECOMMENDED DOSE
50mg: Similar efficacy to 15mg but with increased adverse events (effect size: 0.54, p=0.0069)

SAFETY BY DOSE
Treatment-emergent adverse events increased with dose:
- 10mg: 17% of patients
- 15mg: 34% of patients
- 50mg: 52% of patients
- Placebo: 17% of patients

RECOMMENDATION
The optimal dose of Lisinopril for Hypertension is 15mg twice daily, providing the best balance of efficacy and tolerability. Dose adjustment may be needed for patients with hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B or C).
