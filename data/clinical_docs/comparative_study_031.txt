COMPARATIVE EFFECTIVENESS STUDY
Document ID: CES-0031
Study Title: Head-to-Head Comparison of Metformin vs Amlodipine for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

BACKGROUND
Both Metformin and Amlodipine are approved for the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. This study aimed to compare their relative efficacy and safety in a real-world clinical setting.

STUDY DESIGN
Multicenter, randomized, active-controlled, open-label trial. 2334 patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus were randomized 1:1 to Metformin or Amlodipine for 36 weeks.

PATIENT BASELINE CHARACTERISTICS
- Mean age: 59 years
- Mean BMI: 27.2 kg/m²
- Mean disease duration: 4.5 years
- Prior treatment failure: 36%
- Concomitant medications: 48% on at least one

PRIMARY RESULTS
Metformin: Effect size 0.41 (95% CI [0.31, 0.51])
Amlodipine: Effect size 0.86 (95% CI [0.76, 0.96])

Between-group difference: 0.45 (p=<0.001)

Amlodipine demonstrated superior efficacy compared to Metformin on the primary endpoint.

SAFETY COMPARISON
Treatment discontinuation due to adverse events:
- Metformin: 8.7%
- Amlodipine: 11.1%

Notable differences in adverse event profiles:
- Metformin was associated with higher rates of headache (8.1% vs 2.8%)
- Amlodipine was associated with higher rates of joint pain (12.3% vs 1.4%)

CONCLUSION
In this head-to-head comparison, Amlodipine demonstrated statistically significant superiority over Metformin for the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. The safety profiles of both drugs were generally acceptable, though they differed in specific adverse event patterns. Treatment selection should consider individual patient factors and tolerability preferences.
