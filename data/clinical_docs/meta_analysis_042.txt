META-ANALYSIS REPORT
Document ID: MAR-0042
Title: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Lisinopril for Hypertension

OBJECTIVE
To synthesize evidence from randomized controlled trials (RCTs) evaluating the efficacy and safety of Lisinopril in patients with Hypertension.

SEARCH STRATEGY
Electronic databases (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library) were searched from inception to September 2023. 963 records were screened, and 10 RCTs meeting inclusion criteria were included in the final analysis.

INCLUDED STUDIES
- Number of RCTs: 10
- Total patients: 11109
- Study duration range: 9 to 83 weeks
- Phase II trials: 78%
- Geographic coverage: 50 countries

POOLED RESULTS
Primary Outcome:
- Pooled effect size (random-effects model): 0.58 (95% CI [0.25, 0.77])
- p-value: <0.001
- I² heterogeneity: 32% (substantial)
- Number needed to treat (NNT): 17

Safety Outcomes:
- Risk of serious adverse events: OR 1.23 (95% CI [0.74, 1.59])
- Treatment discontinuation: OR 1.48 (95% CI [0.87, 1.9])

SUBGROUP ANALYSES
- Age ≥65 years: Similar benefit (interaction p=0.33)
- Severe disease at baseline: Greater benefit (effect size: 0.83)
- Prior treatment failure: Maintained efficacy (effect size: 0.39)

CONCLUSION
This meta-analysis of 10 RCTs involving 11109 patients provides moderate evidence supporting the efficacy of Lisinopril for Hypertension. The safety profile is acceptable though ongoing surveillance is warranted.

QUALITY ASSESSMENT
- Risk of bias: Low (Cochrane RoB 2 tool)
- GRADE certainty of evidence: Moderate
- Publication bias: Not detected (Egger test p=0.36)
