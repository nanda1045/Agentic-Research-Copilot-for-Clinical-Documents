DOSE-FINDING STUDY REPORT
Document ID: DFS-0024
Study Title: Dose-Response Evaluation of Metformin in Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

OBJECTIVE
To determine the optimal therapeutic dose of Metformin for the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus by evaluating efficacy and safety across multiple dose levels.

METHODS
This was a Phase IIb dose-ranging study. 714 patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus were randomized to receive Metformin at doses of 10mg, 15mg, or 200mg daily, or placebo, for 8 weeks.

PHARMACOKINETIC PROFILE
Metformin demonstrated linear pharmacokinetics across the dose range studied:
- Tmax: 2.7 hours
- Half-life: 5.9 hours
- Bioavailability: 93%
- Steady-state achieved by Day 4
- No significant food effect observed

DOSE-RESPONSE RESULTS
10mg: Modest improvement over placebo (effect size: 0.19, p=0.062)
15mg: Significant improvement (effect size: 0.65, p=0.0055) — RECOMMENDED DOSE
200mg: Similar efficacy to 15mg but with increased adverse events (effect size: 0.58, p=0.0056)

SAFETY BY DOSE
Treatment-emergent adverse events increased with dose:
- 10mg: 19% of patients
- 15mg: 33% of patients
- 200mg: 50% of patients
- Placebo: 17% of patients

RECOMMENDATION
The optimal dose of Metformin for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus is 15mg once daily, providing the best balance of efficacy and tolerability. Dose adjustment may be needed for patients with hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B or C).
