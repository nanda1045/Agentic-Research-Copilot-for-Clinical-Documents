CLINICAL TRIAL REPORT
Document ID: CTR-0011
Study Title: Phase III Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Sitagliptin for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

STUDY OVERVIEW
This was a phase iii, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial evaluating the efficacy and safety of Sitagliptin in patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. The study was conducted across 70 clinical sites in 15 countries.

PATIENT POPULATION
A total of 2660 patients were enrolled and randomized 1:1 to Sitagliptin or placebo. Key inclusion criteria included age 18-75 years with confirmed diagnosis of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. The mean age of participants was 54 years, with 56% female patients. 74% of patients were White, 15% Black or African American, and 12% Hispanic or Latino.

STUDY DESIGN
Patients received Sitagliptin at a dose of 250mg once daily or matching placebo for 52 weeks. The primary endpoint was HbA1c reduction from baseline (primary: 1.2% vs placebo). Secondary endpoints included safety assessments, quality of life measures (SF-36), and time to first clinical event.

RESULTS
Primary Endpoint: Sitagliptin significantly reduced the primary endpoint compared to placebo (p=0.0137, effect size=0.65, 95% CI [0.55, 0.78]).

The treatment group showed a mean change from baseline of -0.39 compared to -0.06 in the placebo group.

Safety Profile:
The most common adverse events in the Sitagliptin group were:
- hypoglycemia: 9.1% (vs 5.1% placebo)
- constipation: 1.0% (vs 0.4% placebo)
- insomnia: 2.7% (vs 1.5% placebo)

Serious adverse events occurred in 3.5% of the Sitagliptin group vs 0.8% of the placebo group. 1 deaths occurred during the study, none considered related to the study drug.

CONCLUSION
Sitagliptin demonstrated statistically significant efficacy in the treatment of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. The safety profile of Sitagliptin was consistent with prior studies, with no new safety signals identified. Based on these results, Sitagliptin represents a promising therapeutic option for patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus.
