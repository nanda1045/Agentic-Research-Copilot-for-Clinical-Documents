META-ANALYSIS REPORT
Document ID: MAR-0043
Title: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Atorvastatin for Hyperlipidemia

OBJECTIVE
To synthesize evidence from randomized controlled trials (RCTs) evaluating the efficacy and safety of Atorvastatin in patients with Hyperlipidemia.

SEARCH STRATEGY
Electronic databases (PubMed, Embase, Cochrane Library) were searched from inception to March 2024. 1348 records were screened, and 16 RCTs meeting inclusion criteria were included in the final analysis.

INCLUDED STUDIES
- Number of RCTs: 16
- Total patients: 42624
- Study duration range: 12 to 87 weeks
- Phase II trials: 79%
- Geographic coverage: 49 countries

POOLED RESULTS
Primary Outcome:
- Pooled effect size (random-effects model): 0.63 (95% CI [0.3, 0.76])
- p-value: <0.001
- I² heterogeneity: 27% (substantial)
- Number needed to treat (NNT): 13

Safety Outcomes:
- Risk of serious adverse events: OR 1.26 (95% CI [0.67, 1.27])
- Treatment discontinuation: OR 1.11 (95% CI [0.93, 1.55])

SUBGROUP ANALYSES
- Age ≥65 years: Similar benefit (interaction p=0.64)
- Severe disease at baseline: Greater benefit (effect size: 0.99)
- Prior treatment failure: Maintained efficacy (effect size: 0.34)

CONCLUSION
This meta-analysis of 16 RCTs involving 42624 patients provides strong evidence supporting the efficacy of Atorvastatin for Hyperlipidemia. The safety profile is acceptable though ongoing surveillance is warranted.

QUALITY ASSESSMENT
- Risk of bias: Moderate (Cochrane RoB 2 tool)
- GRADE certainty of evidence: High
- Publication bias: Not detected (Egger test p=0.42)
